# Review of a Caption

![Review of a Caption](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Gallery_caption_label.jpg)

The caption used one verb that saved the painting from sentimentality: traded. Not loved, not remembered, not inspired. Traded. Suddenly the soft landscape had an economy.

Captions are small criticism with institutional stationery. They can flatten a work, rescue it, or reveal that the curator knows exactly where the pressure belongs.

Glasswing needs pieces at this scale because a real magazine notices small editorial acts.
