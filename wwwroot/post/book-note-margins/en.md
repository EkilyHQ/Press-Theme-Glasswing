# Book Note: The Margins Did the Work

![Book Note: The Margins Did the Work](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Manuscript_page_with_marginal_notes.jpg)

The fictional book under review argues that margins are where readers become visible. The claim sounds precious until the facsimiles begin to accumulate: grocery sums, corrections, flowers pressed too flat to identify, names repeated until the ink thins.

What I liked was the restraint. The book does not romanticize every annotation. Some margins are dull. Some are wrong. Some are only proof that a reader owned a pencil.

This article gives the demo a lower-stakes format between the lead essay and the archive note. A real review site needs these middle pieces. Not every post can arrive wearing a headline suit.

Best page: the spread where the printed argument is ordinary and the reader's disagreement is electric.
