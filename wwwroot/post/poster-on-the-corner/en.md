# Poster on the Corner

![Poster on the Corner](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Vintage_poster_wall.jpg)

The poster won the corner by refusing to shout. One image, one date, one line of type, and enough blank paper to make the bakery window beside it look noisy.

Street posters are useful tests for editorial judgment because the reader is moving. If the argument cannot survive a three-second glance, the layout is doing too much.

Glasswing handles this format well: short, visual, opinionated, and still sourced.
